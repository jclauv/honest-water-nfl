FAQ

Common Questions

What is a whole-home water filtration system? +
A whole-home water filtration system filters the water at the main point of entry, ensuring clean, filtered water is available throughout your entire home for drinking, cooking, bathing, and cleaning.
How does reverse osmosis work? +
Reverse osmosis pushes water through a semi-permeable membrane that removes up to 99% of contaminants including heavy metals, chemicals, bacteria, and dissolved solids — leaving you with pure, great-tasting water.
How often should I replace the filters? +
Filter replacement schedules vary by system and usage, but most whole-home systems require maintenance every 6–12 months. Our team will walk you through the specific schedule for your system at installation.
Why should I choose a water filtration system for my home? +
Filtered water protects your family's health, extends the life of appliances, makes skin and hair softer, and eliminates the cost and waste of bottled water. Florida's water is notorious for hardness, chlorine, and contaminants — filtration is essential.
What contaminants does your filtration system remove? +
Our systems remove chlorine, chloramines, lead, arsenic, nitrates, bacteria, hard minerals (calcium, magnesium), sediment, VOCs, and hundreds of other contaminants — all certified by NSF standards.
Will the filtration system affect my water pressure? +
No. Our systems are engineered to maintain your home's normal water pressure. In many cases, removing sediment and buildup actually improves pressure throughout your plumbing.
